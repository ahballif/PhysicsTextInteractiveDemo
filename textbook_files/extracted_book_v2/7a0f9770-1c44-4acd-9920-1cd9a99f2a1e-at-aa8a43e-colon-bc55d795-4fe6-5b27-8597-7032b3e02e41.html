93.

The symmetry of the system forces E→ to be perpendicular to the sheet and constant over any plane parallel to the sheet. To calculate the electric field, we choose the cylindrical Gaussian surface shown. The cross-section area and the height of the cylinder are A and 2x, respectively, and the cylinder is positioned so that it is bisected by the plane sheet. Since E is perpendicular to each end and parallel to the side of the cylinder, we have EA as the flux through each end and there is no flux through the side. The charge enclosed by the cylinder is σA, so from Gauss’s law, 2EA=σAε0, and the electric field of an infinite sheet of charge is
 E=σ2ε0, in agreement with the calculation of in the text.
